Intent, Implementation and Consequences

Digital Impact

2 week project: 18.03 - 29.03
Work in groups of 2

Background

It is important to reflect on our responsibility when designing products and services.
This module aims to enhance your ability in predicting different types of use and abuse of your designs, and foresee further potential consequences that might arise.

The task

Design an interface and its interactions for a story, taking place in a near future. Use the interface as a driver in a short film.

The starting point of you work will be either
a) an emerging technology

or

b) a technology instigated user behavior

Examples of emerging technologies

  • Machine learning
    • New data analysis (audio, images and video)
    • Automation
    • Rule based evolution (e.g. ‘AlphaGo’)
    • Human impersonation
  • Blockchain and cryptocurrency
  • Augmented reality
  • Encryption
  • ‘Behavior diagnostics’

Examples of user behavior

  • Selfies
  • Shaming / up-skirting / trolling / online harrasment
  • Sousveillance (cameras and microphones in every inch of our society)
  • Online dating
  • Private fundraising (e.g. GoFundMe)
  • Attention diversion

    • Context for your project

    Existing or new infrastructure

    Invent a new, or piggyback an existing, software that encompass your concept – emotionally, socially, economically, or other. The software will have a UI that you will design the relevant parts of.

    Timeframes: What future?

    It could be tomorrow or next week, as an alternative present. Or, it might play out further into the near future, and even have a timeline that spans over multiple years. Don’t make any projections beyond year 2022.

    Users and usage

    Design not only for primary users but also regarding non-users, group dynamics, and the society as a whole.
    Don’t develop the regular user scenarios where a single user uses a product and get super happy. Choose stress cases that shows where the edges of your project go. It can support ‘so and so’ - but not beyond this point etc.

    Tools

    Research

    Depending on your chosen project be resourceful in how you choose to study your topic.

    Concept development (suggestions)

    Discussions, paper prototyping, clickable’s, wireframes, video sketching, evidencing.

    Filmmaking

    Storytelling, mood setting, camera usage, editing, pace, framing, animation, timing.

    Deliveries

    1. A short film portraying your concept
    2. Short description of your concept (max 280 characters)
    3. ‘Killer image’
    4. Oral presentation.
      Start by showing your short, then dissect and explain relevant thoughts, process and choices.

    Tips & considerations

    • It doesn’t have to be dystopic. You can very well choose to make a net zero outcome, or even make a better world (latter is often harder).
    • If your starting point is technology, research technologies that are approachable and not presented as ‘magical black boxes’.
    • If your starting point is ‘behavior’, study behaviors that has developed from some of our ‘relatively new technologies’, like specific online behavior, or behavior ‘IRL’ (i.e. related to mobile usage).
    • Foresee stress cases: Malintent, misuage, accidental usage, bad implementation, very different users - or adoption of, the same software.
    • Don’t focus on device(s). Hardware is not interesting in this task.
    • Reflect on the normative. (Like a stand-up comedian’s “What’s this thing with […]”)
    • Be efficient when producing, only design and include the relevant bits of the picture.
    • Acting is an art; So be creative to circumvent it. Focus on context and usage. There don’t need to be much of a ‘plot line’ to communicate a project – poor acting or poor plot lines distracts the quality of your concept.
    • The scope of the implications shouldn’t necessarily be on a macro level (e.g. disrupting democracy).
    • The pendulum swings back and forth:
      Technology developed for new behaviors / Behaviors developed from new technology
      Public / Private
      Inner motivation / Gamification

    Evaluation

    Research and insight

    The ability to be creative and resourceful in how you study behavior or emerging technology

    Concept development

    Your ability to drive a strong concept development process. For there to be a good story to tell, there need to be a strong concept.

    Storytelling

    The ability to communicate your project.
